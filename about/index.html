The Case for Clinical Longevity

The case for clinical
longevity.

Most people treat decline as inevitable. A slower metabolism, less energy, harder recovery — they call it aging and they accept it. We don't. The human body is not a fixed system trending toward failure. It is a dynamic one, responsive to inputs — and the clinical tools now available to support its function are more precise than anything that existed a decade ago.

The treatments AnthologyRX prescribes are not shortcuts. They are the same compounds that board-certified physicians use in clinical practice — prescribed after provider review, compounded by licensed 503B pharmacies, managed by real doctors. What makes AnthologyRX different is that we believe these tools should be accessible to anyone who qualifies, not just those with concierge medicine relationships or the right zip code. The standard of care that used to require a $30,000-a-year membership should be available to anyone with a legitimate clinical need and the discipline to follow a protocol.

Using medicine to extend your vitality isn't weakness. It's the same logic as training, nutrition, sleep, and supplementation — just more precise. Athletes track macros. High performers optimize sleep. The next iteration of that discipline is clinical. AnthologyRX exists to destigmatize that choice and make it clinically sound, provider-supervised, and accessible.

The People Behind the Protocols

The people behind

Four founders with skin in the game — literally.

ME
Michael Eisman
Co-Founder & CEO

Michael Eisman has built and scaled businesses across some of the most operationally complex industries in modern commerce. At Apple, he internalized what operational excellence looks like at true scale. At MedMen, he helped co-create what the press called "the Apple Store of Weed" — an ambitious national cannabis retail concept that raised $150M and became one of the first recognized consumer brands in a nascent industry. When that chapter closed, he took on the turnaround of a distressed B2B logistics company, eliminated $2M in inherited debt, and sold the business for 8x EBITDA.

Most recently, Michael founded Echelon Agency, a talent management firm representing fitness and lifestyle creators in Los Angeles — putting him at the intersection of elite performance culture and the creator economy. That proximity gave him an early read on what would become AnthologyRX: creators and executives around him were quietly relying on clinical longevity treatments to sustain the physical and mental demands of their lives, but had no consumer-grade, clinically credible way to access them. The category was real. The access wasn't.

Michael personally relies on several of the protocols AnthologyRX offers. For him, this is not a market opportunity — it is a daily reality that he decided to build the infrastructure around.

DW
Danika Lane Wignall
Co-Founder & President

Danika Lane Wignall has spent more than a decade building and leading high-performance B2B sales organizations at the VP level. She is a single mother of two who runs a demanding corporate career without the luxury of slowing down — which means that energy, focus, and physical health are not aspirational priorities. They are operational ones. She relies on several AnthologyRX protocols to sustain the output her life requires, and she has the receipts to prove they work.

Danika brings something to AnthologyRX that the category desperately needed: the female perspective. Longevity and metabolic optimization are too often framed as the province of biohacking men in their forties. But NAD+, GLP-1, and PT-141 are equally relevant to a woman managing perimenopause, a demanding career, school pickups, and a fitness practice she refuses to abandon. The market has been underserving this person. Danika is that person, and she is building for her.

Her insight was simple: if a working single mother running a VP-level sales team can benefit from these protocols — and does — then the addressable audience for clinical longevity medicine is far wider than the industry has acknowledged. She is here to close that gap.

DR
Dr. Dylan Russell, MD
Co-Founder & Chief Medical Officer

Dr. Dylan Russell graduated from West Point with a BS in Life Sciences and Chemistry in 2013, then earned his MD from the University of Virginia School of Medicine in 2017. He served as a U.S. Army General Surgeon — trained at Tripler Army Medical Center — before transitioning to advanced civilian surgical training. He is currently a Transplant Surgery Fellow in the Advanced Care for Liver Disease and Transplant Surgery Program at Keck Hospital of USC and the Keck School of Medicine, where he is a published researcher with peer-reviewed work cited over 176 times.

A career at the intersection of military surgery and academic transplant medicine gives Dr. Russell a particular vantage point on prevention. He has seen up close what late-stage metabolic disease and physiological decline look like — the liver failure, the cardiovascular deterioration, the outcomes that arrive at the end of decades of unaddressed decline. His conviction that prevention is the only rational strategy is not philosophical. It is clinical.

As Chief Medical Officer, Dr. Russell provides medical oversight and protocol review for AnthologyRX. Every compound the company prescribes is vetted through the lens of clinical evidence, provider safety standards, and the same intellectual rigor he applies to his surgical and research work. AnthologyRX protocols are designed to meet the standard a transplant surgeon would accept for his own patients — and himself.

DS
Dave Schenk
Co-Founder, Chief Brand Officer & Spokesman

Dave Schenk has lived in the gym his entire adult life. He was a Division I wrestler at Cal Poly San Luis Obispo — team captain and conference champion at 197 lbs — and has spent the 20-plus years since building and leading fitness communities. He is ACE and NASM certified, co-founded LIFT Society in Los Angeles (2016–2024), launched Iron Anthology gym in 2024, and has presented at international fitness conferences including ASIAFIT in Shanghai, where he led sessions on cross-training, kettlebell sport, and bodyweight training to audiences of 300 or more.

At 46, Dave has earned the right to be honest about what happens to the body over time — even for people who have done everything right. Hormone production changes. Metabolic efficiency shifts. Visceral fat accumulates in places it never used to, despite the same training discipline that defined the previous two decades. He is beginning his own Tesamorelin protocol to address exactly that — not as a shortcut, but as the same precision thinking he has applied to programming, periodization, and recovery his entire career.

Dave's philosophy is simple: using the appropriate clinical tools to extend the body's ability to stay in motion is not cheating. It is discipline applied at a higher resolution. He is the face of AnthologyRX because he is its most literal embodiment — a lifelong athlete who has decided that sustaining performance across decades is worth using every evidence-based tool available.
