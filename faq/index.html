Medical & Safety
Semaglutide (the active compound in Ozempic and Wegovy) and tirzepatide (active compound in Mounjaro and Zepbound) are both FDA-approved drugs. The versions dispensed through AnthologyRX are compounded formulations produced by a licensed 503B compounding pharmacy operating under FDA oversight and cGMP standards — not manufactured by Novo Nordisk or Eli Lilly, but containing the same active compounds.
The most common side effects are gastrointestinal: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation. These typically peak in the first several weeks and diminish as the body adjusts — slow dose titration is specifically designed to minimize their severity.

More serious risks — including pancreatitis, gallbladder disease, and a theoretical association with thyroid C-cell tumors (observed in rodent studies, not confirmed in humans) — are real and are screened for during intake. Patients with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN2 are not eligible. Your provider reviews every intake specifically to identify these contraindications.
No more than using a knee brace after an injury is "cheating" at physical therapy. Metabolic dysregulation has physiological underpinnings — hormonal, genetic, and neurological — that diet and exercise alone frequently cannot override. GLP-1 agonists work by correcting a signaling deficit: your brain receives the satiety signals it's supposed to, and food noise diminishes accordingly. That's not circumventing effort. That's removing a structural obstacle to effort.
