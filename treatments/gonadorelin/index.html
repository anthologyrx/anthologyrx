What It Is

Your body's own
hormone signal — amplified.

Gonadorelin is a synthetic gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) analogue. It works by pulsing the pituitary gland to release luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) — the upstream signals that drive the testes to produce testosterone and maintain sperm production.

The critical distinction: Gonadorelin does not introduce exogenous testosterone. It stimulates your body's own production through the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis — the natural regulatory pathway that controls male hormone balance. Unlike TRT, it works with your biology rather than overriding it.

For men experiencing symptoms of low testosterone — persistent fatigue, reduced drive, changes in body composition — who want hormonal support without the suppression and shutdown risk of exogenous testosterone, Gonadorelin is a provider-guided path forward. Common Questions

What you need to know.

TRT (testosterone replacement therapy) introduces exogenous testosterone — the body reads this external supply as sufficient and progressively shuts down its own production through HPG axis suppression. Over time, LH and FSH output declines, testicular volume may decrease, and the body's ability to produce testosterone naturally is diminished. Gonadorelin works upstream: it stimulates the pituitary to release LH and FSH, which in turn signal the testes to produce testosterone endogenously. The body's own regulatory axis stays active. This is the key clinical distinction. TRT replaces. Gonadorelin preserves and stimulates. Your provider will advise which approach is appropriate for your lab values and goals.

Gonadorelin is specifically a fertility-conscious option. Because it stimulates LH and FSH — the hormones that drive sperm production — it maintains the pathway that TRT typically suppresses. Men on exogenous testosterone commonly experience significant reductions in sperm count and testicular function over time. Gonadorelin keeps the HPG axis intact, making it an appropriate option for men who wish to preserve fertility potential while addressing hormonal symptoms. That said, any hormone-modulating intervention should be discussed fully with your provider. Your physician will review your reproductive health goals as part of candidacy evaluation.

Most patients report initial changes within 4–8 weeks — commonly improved energy, mood stabilization, and early shifts in motivation and drive. Body composition and libido changes typically emerge over a longer arc: 8–16 weeks. Because Gonadorelin works by stimulating your body's own production rather than delivering synthetic testosterone directly, the response timeline is more gradual than exogenous hormone replacement. Lab follow-up at 8–12 weeks confirms whether testosterone, LH, and FSH levels are responding as expected. Individual results vary significantly. This is not a guaranteed timeline.
