What It Is

Precision pharmacology.
Not a program.

GLP-1 receptor agonists — semaglutide and tirzepatide — represent the most clinically significant advance in metabolic medicine in a generation. These are not supplements or lifestyle tools. They are physician-prescribed compounds that directly modulate appetite signaling, insulin sensitivity, and fat metabolism at the cellular level.

GLP-1 stands for glucagon-like peptide-1, a hormone naturally produced in the gut after eating. By mimicking and extending this signal, these compounds slow gastric emptying, reduce glucagon secretion, and act on hypothalamic receptors to suppress food noise — the background cognitive preoccupation with eating that undermines even the most disciplined approaches.
