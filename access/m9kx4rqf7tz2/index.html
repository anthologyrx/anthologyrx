Section 02 · Interest

How your capital earns.

01
Fixed Rate
12.00% per annum on the outstanding principal balance, calculated on a 365-day year.
02
Annual Compounding
Each anniversary of the Effective Date, accrued interest is added to principal and begins earning interest itself.
03
Tranche-Specific
Interest on each advance accrues from its own date, not the original Effective Date. Every dollar starts earning immediately.

A $10,000 advance at 12% compounded annually grows to approximately $17,623 after 5 years. At maturity (year 7), it reaches approximately $22,107.

Section 03 · Repayment

How you get repaid.

Repayment scales with the company's financial health. The structure prevents premature cash extraction while ensuring you are paid back as soon as the business genuinely can.

<6 months reserves
Voluntary repayment with unanimous consent
If a founder demonstrates financial need, the Members may unanimously approve early repayment of that founder's loan. A guardrail with flexibility — protection from capricious withdrawal, allowance for genuine hardship.
6+ months reserves
Mandatory quarterly interest payments begin
Once the company sustains six months of operating reserves for two consecutive quarters, accrued interest is paid pro rata to all founder loan holders every quarter.
9+ months reserves
Mandatory quarterly principal payments begin
Once the company sustains nine months of reserves for two consecutive quarters, principal repayment begins, pro rata across all founder loans. Cash flows back to founders systematically as the business strengthens.
07 year maturity
Backstop: full repayment due
Any outstanding principal and accrued interest must be paid in full on the 7th anniversary of the Effective Date, regardless of where reserves stand.
Sections 04 & 05 · Subordination & Recourse

Where you stand in line.

The note's priority and recourse structure exists to protect the company's operational integrity, ensure pro rata treatment among founder lenders, and shield individual founders from personal liability.

→
Senior to equity distributions
Founder loans are repaid before any equity distribution to Members (tax distributions excepted).
=
Pari passu with peer loans
All founder loans rank equally. Repayment is pro rata. No founder is preferred over another.
↓
Subordinated where it matters
Trade payables, employee compensation, taxes, and future institutional financing are paid first. Operations protected.
●
No personal recourse
If the company dissolves with insufficient assets, the unpaid balance is uncollectible. No founder is personally liable.
For Clarity

What this note is not.

It is not equity. Repayment satisfies the obligation in full. There is no conversion right, no participation in upside, no claim on the company's equity value beyond what is owed in principal and interest.

It is not a personal obligation of the founders. The note is solely an obligation of AnthologyRX LLC. The limited liability of the LLC structure protects every Member and signatory.

It is not callable on demand. The repayment triggers are objective and tied to operating reserves. Neither party can unilaterally accelerate repayment outside the defined events of default.
